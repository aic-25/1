Bracelet tutorial

To make a bracelet like the one in the picture you will need the following:

  • Beads of your choice
  • A clasp
  • Many charms
  • Jumprings
  • Crimp & Needle Nose Pliers
  • Cutters
  • Eyepins 3cm (preferably)

First we will start by grabbing the eyepins and using the needle nose pliers to open them to insert the beads and charms, then using the crimp pliers to close them. Once this is done we will also do the same thing but use the jump rings instead and insert small charms that fit into them and with the pliers close them. After having everything done we just need to put it together, so once again using the needle nose and crimp pliers you will connect all the eye heads with the beads inside them in a way that creates the design you want while also adding the jump rings with the beads to the eye heads. The picture above can be used as a reference for you to follow a pattern. Once the main part is connected, you simply need to close it by adding the clasp at one of the ends of the bracelet using the pliers and connecting it with a jump ring. On the other end of the bracelet, you would need to add a jump ring (a bigger one if available) and then you will have created a bracelet all by yourself!

Earring tutorial

Making earrings is also a fun jewelry project to try, and it's great for beginners as it's simple and less time-consuming! Here are the materials you will need:

  • 2 beads
  • 2 charms
  • 2 earring hooks
  • Two earring backs
  • Jump rings
  • Needle Nose Pliers
  • Cutters (optional)
  • Eyepins (preferably) or headpins 2cm
  • Cable chain (preferably thin)

To start off, you will grab the bead/charm of your choice and insert it into an eyepin using the pliers to open and close the pin. Then, you will use the jump rings instead and insert a bead/charm into each jump ring, opening and closing them with the pliers. After this, you should have four pieces in total. Next, you can cut some cable chain with the cutters and add a jump ring on each side using the pliers to open and close them. Then, connect one side to the eyepin you previously worked on while on the other side, add the previously worked-on jump ring with the bead/charm inside it. Finally, you should now have two pieces to which you will add an earring hook using jump rings and pliers. Now you're done and have made earrings!
